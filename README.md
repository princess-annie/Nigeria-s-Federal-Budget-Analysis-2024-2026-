## The Budget of Survival - Nigeria Federal Budget Analysis

## Project Overview
This project delivers a data-driven investigative report on Nigeria's federal budget allocations between 2024 and 2026, Using PostgreSQL for data cleaning and transformation and Power BI for interactive visualisation,
the analysis investigates one central question: Nigeria is spending more every year, but are Nigerians actually living better?
Drawing on five datasets: 
1. federal budget allocations
2. public debt records, CBN inflation data,
3. WFP food price indices, and NBS socioeconomic indicators — the report traces ₦107.2 trillion in combined federal spending and finds that budget growth is stucked at debt and not the people

## Problem Statement
Nigeria's 2026 federal budget reached ₦51.6 trillion, its largest single-year allocation in history. Yet food prices remain above pre-crisis levels, the minimum wage has not changed since 2024,
and the national poverty rate has not fallen. The data reveals a structural problem: half the budget goes to debt before a single naira reaches a classroom, hospital, or household.
The question this analysis answers is not how much Nigeria is spending, it is what that spending is actually doing for Nigerians.
